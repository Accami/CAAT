One of the minimum CAAT library setup programs could be this one: 

1    function __CAAT_tut1_init() {
2    
3        var director = new CAAT.Director().initialize(100,100);
4        var scene=     new CAAT.Scene().create();
5        var circle=    new CAAT.ShapeActor().create().setLocation(20,20).setSize(60,60).setFillStyle('#ff0000').setStrokeStyle('#000000');
6    
7        scene.addChild(circle);
8        director.addScene(scene);
9    
10       var canvasContainer= document.createElement('div');
11       canvasContainer.appendChild( director.canvas );
12       document.body.appendChild(canvasContainer);
13   
14       director.loop(1);
15   }
16   
17   window.addEventListener('load', __CAAT_tut1_init, false);

By executing it, we'll get a black-outlined red circle at position 20,20 and of radius 30.

Despite its simplicity, some facts must be taken into account:

  1. When using CAAT, it's mandatory to create a CAAT.Director instance. The Director is the entry point to CAAT.
  2. The Director instance is fed with scenes. CAAT.Scene instances are advanced actor containers. We'll see how the Director manages Scenes, and how to elegantly switch from one to the other.
  3. Scenes are fed with CAAT.Actor instances. Indeed, as much as needed.
  4. CAAT.Actor instances and its subinstances (ShapeActor, SpriteActor, etc.) have a lifecycle, so it must be explicitly stated the call to create method.
  5. We must call director.loop to signal CAAT to start delivering content to the canvas.
